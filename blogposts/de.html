Homogeneous and non-homogeneous higher order ordinary differential equations

Mohammad R. Islam

2/26/2021

Example 1

Solve the ODE below:
\[\begin{align*} x^{(4)}-5x''+4x&=0 \end{align*}\]

Solution:

The characteristic equation and its solution is given as
\[\begin{align*} \lambda^4-5\lambda^2+4&=0\\ \implies (\lambda^2-4)(\lambda^2-1)&=0\\ \implies (\lambda^2-4)&=0 & (\lambda^2-1)&=0\\ \implies \lambda&=\pm 2 & \lambda&=\pm 1 \end{align*}\]

Therefore, the solution of the given ODE is

\[\begin{align*} x(t)&=c_1e^{-t}+c_2e^{t}+c_3e^{-2t}+c_4e^{2t} & c_1,\cdots ,c_4 \in \mathbb{R} \end{align*}\]

Example 2

Solve the ODE below:
\[\begin{equation*} x''+2x'+10x=0 \end{equation*}\]

Solution:

The characteristic equation and its solution is given as
\[\begin{align*} \lambda^2+2\lambda+10&=0\\ \implies (\lambda+1)^2&=-9\\ \implies \lambda&=-1\pm 3i \end{align*}\]

Therefore, the solution of the given ODE is

\[\begin{align*} x(t)&=c_1e^{-t}\cos{3t}+c_2e^{-t}\sin{3t}; & c_1,c_2 \in \mathbb{R} \end{align*}\]

Example 3

Solve the ODE below:
\[\begin{align*} \frac{d^2x}{dt^2}-a^2x&=t+1 \end{align*}\]

Solution:

The characteristic equation of the homogeneous ODE \(\frac{d^2x}{dt^2}-a^2x=0\) is \(\lambda^2-a^2=0\) and its roots are \(\lambda_1=a,\lambda_2=-a\). Hence the complementary solution of the ODE is
\[\begin{align*} x_c&=c_1e^a+c_2e^{-a}& c_1,c_2 \in \mathbb{R} \end{align*}\]

Since for the non-homogeneous part \(\lambda=0\) and \(0\) is not a solution of the characteristic equation hence \(k=0\). So the particular solution has the form \(x_p=A+Bt\). Then
\[\begin{align*} \frac{dx_p}{dt}&=B& \frac{d^2x_p}{dt^2}=0 \end{align*}\]

Plugging these values we have \(-a^2(A+Bt)=t+1\). That is \(-a^2A=1 \implies A=-\frac{1}{a^2}\) and \(-a^2B=1\implies B=-\frac{1}{a^2}\) if \(a\ne 0\). So \(x_p=-\frac{1}{a^2}(1+t)\). Therefore, the solution of the given ODE is
\[\begin{align*} x=x_c+x_p&\implies x=c_1e^a+c_2e^{-a}-\frac{1}{a^2}(1+t);& c_1,c_2 \in \mathbb{R}, a\ne 0 \end{align*}\]

Example 4

Solve the ODE below:
\[\begin{align*} \frac{d^3x}{dt^3}+3\frac{d^2x}{dt^2}+3\frac{dx}{dt}+x&=e^{-t}(t-5) \end{align*}\]

Solution:

The characteristic equation of the homogeneous ODE \(\frac{d^3x}{dt^3}+3\frac{d^2x}{dt^2}+3\frac{dx}{dt}+x=0\) is \((\lambda+1)^3=0\) and its root is \(\lambda=-1\) with multiplicity \(k=3\). Hence the complementary solution is
\[\begin{align*} x_c&=c_1e^{-t}+c_2te^{-t}+c_3t^2e^{-t}; & c_1,c_2,c_3\in \mathbb{R} \end{align*}\]

Now for the non-homogeneous part we see that \(\lambda=-1\) which is also a root of the characteristic equation and \(k=3\). So we can try with the particular solution \(x_p=t^3(At+B)e^{-t}\). Then
\[\begin{align*} \frac{dx_p}{dt}&=(4At^3+3Bt^2)e^{-t}-(At^4+Bt^3)e^{-t}\\ &=(-At^4+(4A-B)t^3+3Bt^2)e^{-t}\\ \frac{d^2x_p}{dt^2}&=(-4At^3+3(4A-B)t^2+6Bt)e^{-t}-(-At^4+(4A-B)t^3+3Bt^2)e^{-t}\\ &=(At^4+(-8A+B)t^3+(12A-6B)t^2+6B)e^{-t}\\ \frac{d^3x_p}{dt^3}&=(4At^3+(-24A+3B)t^2+(24A-12B)t)e^{-t}-(At^4+(-8A+B)t^3+(12A-6B)t^2+6B)e^{-t}\\ &=(-At^4+(12A-B)t^3+(-36A+3B)t^2+(24A-12B)t-6B)e^{-t}\\ \end{align*}\]

Plugging these in the non-homogeneous ODE we have \(A=\frac{1}{24}, B=-\frac{5}{6}\). So \(x_p=\frac{1}{24}t^4e^{-t}-\frac{5}{6}t^3e^{-t}\). Hence the general solution of the given non-homogeneous ODE is
\[\begin{align*} x=x_c+x_p &\implies x=c_1e^{-t}+c_2te^{-t}+c_3t^2e^{-t}+\frac{1}{24}t^4e^{-t}-\frac{5}{6}t^3e^{-t}; & c_1,c_2,c_3\in \mathbb{R} \end{align*}\]
